The Car Collection of Orlando Bloom

Orlando Bloom born on 13 January 1977, is an English actor. He made his breakthrough as the character Legolas in The Lord of the Rings film series. He reprised his role in The Hobbit film series. Considered the Errol Flynn of his time, he gained further notice appearing in epic fantasy, historical, and adventure films, notably as Will Turner in the Pirates of the Caribbean film series. He made his professional stage debut in In Celebration at the Duke of York’s Theatre in the West End in 2007. He returned to the theatre in a West End revival of Tracy Letts’ Killer Joe in 2018. As of now, Orlando Bloom is currently dating American Singer Katy Perry. His estimated net worth stands at $40 Million, with an annual salary of $5 Million. So, check out the cars that Bloom has in his garage.

1. Lucid Air

The Lucid Air is currently the most powerful performance sedan that exists on the market. A carmaker that is not even some years old, has made one of the most powerful and luxurious electric sedans that many carmakers still think to make. In a world with legacy automakers like Porsche and Ford with a decades-long history in making cars, this EV startup aims to rival them with their latest EV. The Lucid Air aims to challenge these big carmakers with its ferocious acceleration, beautiful design, and insane power. The Air Grand Turing Performance offers an immersive and luxurious experience with its mammoth 1,050 hp electric motor design. The Air can accelerate from 0-60 mph in under 2.5 seconds and delivers a driving range of 520 miles.

2. Porsche 912

Porsche started the production of the 912 Coupe in 1965 which continued till 1969. It comes under the category of a sports car and is fitted with a 1.6L flat-4 which generates about 105 hp. With a launching price of only $4,700 at that time, 912 can achieve a top speed of 119 mph and sprint from 0-60 mph in 11 sec.

3. Porsche Cayenne

A beautifully designed exterior, a high-class interior, and an unreal performance that can shake your bones are all you can need from the Porsche Cayenne. The Cayenne is the best option for someone who wants to fulfill their obnoxious thrill sporty driving needs. The Cayenne offers a plethora of powertrain option ranging from a turbo-four to a twin-turbo V8 paired with electric motors that makes a hefty 670 horsepower. The eye-watering specs result in brutal acceleration and a ridiculous performance on road.

4. Porsche Carrera 4 GTS

Porsche 911 is likely the first render in one’s imagination if one closes their eyes and pictures a Porsche. This rear-engined fastback is a legend, and there’s a good reason for that. Not only a reason, make that many reasons. For decades it has been a benchmark for performance and handling and feels. Inspiring rivals such as the Aston Martin Vantage, the Audi R8, and the Maserati MC20. The “standard or base” 911 sticks to its roots with a set of twin-turbo flat-six engines which have been tuned for pumping out up to 473 horsepower. Powering from the rear of the 911 is a twin-turbo 3.0-Liter flat-six-pot engine. The base Carrera makes 379 horsepower, the S pumps out 443-hp, and the GTS generates 473 horses.

5. Audi S7

The S7 makes a sweet spot between the A7 and the most powerful RS7. The new S7 differentiates itself from the A7 with its powerful 335-hp twin-turbocharged V6 engine inside. While the RS7 offers a more powerful V8, the middle causing keeps the refinement from the 4-cylinder engine while offering a thrilling performance similar to the V8. As the S7 shares a lot of its style and interior with the A7, the Audi S7 offers distinctive sweet driving with a lot of athleticism.

6. Audi Q7

The spacious German Orlando Bloom owns in his car collection is the luxurious Audi Q7. The Q7 states the standard prominent design that can be seen in the rest of the lineup with a luxury and feature-rich cabin and three-row seats. The car offers two powertrain options with a 248 hp turbo four. And a 335 hp turbo V6 for the ones who seek more performance with an all-wheel drive system as standard and adjustable air suspension for even more comfort and a seamless driving experience.

7. Ford Bronco

Bronco is a full-size SUV produced by Ford Motor Company. Its production started in the year 1966 and continued till 1996 before Ford stopped its production. It again came into production in 2021. Orlando’s Bronco features a mild neutral color and it comes with a 2.3L turbocharged inline-four that generates 275 hp and a top speed of 120 mph. Its base price is $27,265 and goes from rest to 60 mph in 5.9 sec.
