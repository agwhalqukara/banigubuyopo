Etta James (Blues Singer) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Jamesetta Hawkins, known professionally as Etta James, is an American singer who performs in a variety of genres including blues, R&B, soul, rock and roll, jazz and gospel. Starting her career in 1954, she became known for hits like "The Wallflower", "At Last", "Tell Mama", "Something's Got a Hold on Me" and "I'll Rather Go. Blind ". She faced a number of personal problems, including heroin addiction, serious physical abuse and detention, before returning to music in the late 1980s with her album Seven Year Itch. James' loud, deep and earthy voice has narrowed the gap between rhythm, blues and rock and roll. She has won six Grammy Awards and 17 Blues Music Awards. She was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1993, the Grammy Hall of Fame in 1999, and the Blues Hall of Fame in 2001. Rolling Stone Magazine ranked James 22nd on its 100 Great Singers list. She is also ranked 62nd on the list of the 100 greatest artists of all time.
